Judgment
1. This is an appeal by the Appellant, Yiu Yau Ho Daniel, against his conviction after trial before Magistrate Mr. standard adjudicator at the Eastern Magistracy on a single charge of indecent assault, contrary to section 122(1) of the Crimes Ordinance, Cap. 200.
2. The prosecution case turned essentially on the credibility of the complainant, who testified to the inappropriate touching committed by the Appellant. The Appellant, a young man in his late 20s with a clear record and stable character references, denied the offense, asserting that any contact made was completely accidental and lacked indecent intent within the crowded context of the environment.
3. On appeal, the Appellant advanced multiple grounds challenging the safety of the conviction. It was contended on his behalf that his former trial defense counsel provided incompetent representation, failing to properly deploy crucial character evidence or highlight significant inconsistencies in the complainant's chronological account during cross-examination. Additional materials were submitted de bene esse for the Court’s consideration to demonstrate his unblemished background.
4. Having carefully reviewed the transcript of the trial proceedings, the learned Magistrate's statement of findings, and the submissions made by both appellate counsel, this Court finds no merit in the criticisms leveled at the trial defense strategy. The trial handling fell well within the bounds of reasonable professional competence and judgment.
5. The learned Magistrate had the distinct advantage of seeing and hearing the witnesses firsthand. He provided a meticulous, well-reasoned assessment of the evidence, explicitly noting why he found the complainant to be a compelling, reliable witness while rejecting the Appellant’s version of events as an afterthought. The character references, while valid, could not overcome the direct, credible testimony accepted by the trial court.
6. Consequently, this Court is fully satisfied that the conviction remains entirely safe and satisfactory. The fresh evidence presented does not disturb the underlying foundation of the prosecution's case. The appeal against conviction must therefore be dismissed.

(Michael Lunn)
Judge of the Court of First Instance
